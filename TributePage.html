Babasaheb Ambedkar

A tribute page to the architect of the Indian Constitution

Introduction

Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar (14 April 1891 â€“ 6 December 1956), popularly known as Babasaheb Ambedkar, was an Indian jurist, economist, politician, and social reformer who inspired the Dalit Buddhist Movement and campaigned against social discrimination against Dalits, women, and labour.

Early Life

Ambedkar was born into a poor Mahar family in Mhow, Central Provinces and Berar, India (now in Madhya Pradesh). He was the 14th and last child of Ramji Maloji Sakpal, a military officer who held the rank of Subedar, and Bhimabai Sakpal, daughter of Laxman Murbadkar. His family was of Marathi background from the town of Ambavade (Mandangad taluka) in Ratnagiri district of modern-day Maharashtra.

Accomplishments

Ambedkar was the chief architect of the Indian Constitution, which was adopted in 1950. He is also credited with establishing the Reserve Bank of India as well as the Scheduled Castes Federation, which sought to promote the welfare of Dalits.

Ambedkar was a prolific writer and scholar, with his most notable works including "Annihilation of Caste" and "The Buddha and His Dhamma". He also earned a doctorate in economics from the London School of Economics and was appointed as the first law minister of independent India.

Legacy

Ambedkar's legacy lives on in India today, with many institutions and initiatives dedicated to promoting the welfare of Dalits and other marginalized communities. His tireless advocacy for social justice and equality has inspired generations of Indians to fight against discrimination and oppression.
